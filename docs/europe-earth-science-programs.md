## Part 2: Detailed Profiles

### 1. University of Padua (Italy)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Program** | B.Sc. in Earth and Climate Dynamics |
| **Tuition** | ~€2,739/year (~$4,050 CAD) |
| **Travel** | LCA → Venice (VCE) direct (~3.5 hrs) + 30-min train to Padua |
| **Visa / Entry** | Non-competitive TOLC-I scientific test. Moderate visa bureaucracy post-arrival (*Permesso di Soggiorno*). |

**Highlights**

- One of the oldest universities in the world (est. 1222)
- World-class paleontological collections (the “Hall of Palms”)
- Stable isotope geochemistry applied to archaeology

---

### 2. University of Strasbourg (France)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Program** | Licence in Earth Sciences (Bilingual Stream) |
| **Tuition** | €2,770/year (~$4,050 CAD). Often waived down to €175/year for bilingual applicants. |
| **Travel** | LCA → Frankfurt (FRA) direct (~4 hrs) + 1 hr 45 min TGV or Lufthansa shuttle to Strasbourg |
| **Visa / Entry** | Campus France portal. Smooth digital visa validation on arrival. |

**Highlights**

- Offered by EOST (School and Observatory of Earth Sciences)
- Elite focus on rock petrology, mineralogy, and geochemistry
- High-prestige name in Europe and Canada

---

### 3. University of Hamburg (Germany)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Program** | B.Sc. in Earth System Physics |
| **Tuition** | €0 (≈ $1,000 CAD/year in student fees; includes free national public transit pass) |
| **Travel** | LCA → Hamburg (HAM) direct (~4 hrs 15 mins) + local transit |
| **Visa / Entry** | Canadian citizens can apply for residence permit after arrival. Requires upfront blocked bank account (€11,904). |

**Highlights**

- Quantitative, programming-heavy (Python) approach
- Solid-earth geophysics and climatology
- Strong for future-proofing career options
