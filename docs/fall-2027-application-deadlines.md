## Residence note (Georgia)

France and some other portals follow **country of residence**, not citizenship.

- Georgia is in the **Études en France** / Campus France network.
- French Licence Year 1 applications go through **Campus France Georgia** on the Études en France platform (DAP for first-year bachelor).
- Local contact pattern: Institut français de Géorgie / Campus France Géorgie.
- French proficiency testing for DAP (e.g. TCF DAP) is typically arranged locally — check the Georgia Campus France calendar when the 2027/28 campaign opens.

Canadian citizenship still helps for visas/residence in many EU countries, but **application portals are driven by living in Georgia**.

## How applying before graduation works

Almost all of these schools expect PJ to apply **during Grade 12**, before the OSSD is finished. That is normal.

1. **Apply with what he has** — mid-year / in-progress OVS transcript, list of Grade 12 courses underway, passport, English (and French if required) proof, motivation letter where asked.
2. **Get a conditional offer** — accepted if he finishes the OSSD with the stated average / required subjects.
3. **Send finals later** — after June graduation, upload final transcript + diploma (often July–September). Enrolment and visa/residence usually need that final proof.
4. **Visa / residence timing** — many schools issue a conditional letter first so paperwork can start; final acceptance may wait until tuition deposit + diploma.

### Canada (OUAC and others)

Same pattern: schools use **Grade 11 + mid-year Grade 12**, then update with finals. Offers stay conditional until OVS reports final marks.

### Georgia-specific admin tips

- Keep scans of passport, OVS transcripts, and any language certificates ready before Oct 2026 (France opens early).
- For France: watch **Campus France Georgia** dates for DAP close (~15 Dec) and any local TCF DAP sittings.
- For Schengen study permits: start soon after a usable admission letter; summer bottlenecks are common.
